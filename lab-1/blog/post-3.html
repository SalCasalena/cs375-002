Bison in Yellowstone

Yellowstone National Park is home to the largest and oldest public bison herd in the United States. Here are some key points about the bison in Yellowstone: Population: The Yellowstone bison population fluctuates between 3,000 and 6,000 individuals1. As of 2024, the pre-calving population estimate is around 4,550 bison. Habitat: These bison roam freely across the expansive landscape of Yellowstone and some nearby areas of Montana1. They can be seen year-round in places like the Hayden and Lamar valleys, and during the summer in grasslands. Behavior: Yellowstone bison exhibit wild behaviors similar to their ancient ancestors. They congregate during the breeding season to compete for mates and migrate to find new habitats1. Conservation: Yellowstone is the only place in the lower 48 states where bison have lived continuously since prehistoric times1.
